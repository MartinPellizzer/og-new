To investigate the effects of ozone on fetal brain development, pregnant Sprague-Dawley rats were exposed to either 0.3 ppm of O3 or filtered air (FA) via whole-body inhalation at gestational day (GD) 10 or GD20. Control animals received a sham FA exposure at both time points. Fetal brains were collected at GD21 and processed for unbiased, quantitative mass spectrometry to identify altered protein abundance and posttranslational states.
