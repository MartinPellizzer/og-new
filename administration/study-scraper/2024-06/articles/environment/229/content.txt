**Paragraph 1: Introduction**
The tropical tropopause plays a crucial role in regulating the radiation budget and exchange processes between the troposphere and stratosphere. The characterization of its parameters, such as cold point temperature (CPT-T) and convective tropopause (COT-T), is vital for understanding the impact of deep convection on the tropopause structure. This study aims to investigate the seasonal variations of tropical tropopause layer (TTL) parameters over Chennai, a city located in the northeast monsoon region.

**Paragraph 2: Methods**
The study utilizes radiosonde observations from 2014 to 2019 to characterize the cold point and convective tropopause temperatures, as well as the TTL thickness. The water vapor and ozone data from the microwave limb sounder (MLS) were also used to understand their roles in CPT variations under different convective conditions obtained from Infrared brightness temperature (IRBT) data. The entire study was analyzed using Matlab software.

**Paragraph 3: Results**
The results show that the cold point tropopause (CPT) over Chennai becomes higher and colder during the winter season, while it is lower and warmer during the summer monsoon season. The water vapor and ozone mixing ratios at CPT are found to be lower during the winter season and higher during the summer monsoon season. The TTL thickness is also lesser during the winter and greater during the summer monsoon seasons.

**Paragraph 4: Discussion**
The study's findings suggest that the seasonal variations of tropical tropopause layer parameters over Chennai are influenced by deep convection. The results are in phase with the upper troposphere and lower stratospheric temperature, water vapor, and ozone anomalies. The study also categorizes tropical convective conditions as non-penetrative and penetrative using IRBT data, which helps to understand the impact of deep convection on the TTL.

**Paragraph 5: Conclusions**
In conclusion, this study has characterized the seasonal variations of tropical tropopause layer parameters over Chennai and highlighted the role of deep convection in influencing these variations. The findings have important implications for understanding the radiation budget and exchange processes between the troposphere and stratosphere. Further research is needed to investigate the global extent of these variations and their impacts on climate variability and change.
