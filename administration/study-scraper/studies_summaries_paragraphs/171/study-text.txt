The article discusses the paradoxes surrounding ozone, a molecule that can be both beneficial and detrimental depending on its altitude. At lower altitudes, ozone acts as a protective layer against harmful UV rays, essential for life on Earth. However, at higher altitudes, ozone is considered a greenhouse gas, contributing to climate change. Furthermore, tropospheric ozone (ground-level ozone) is a major air pollutant, harmful to human health and crops. The study highlights the dual nature of ozone, emphasizing the need for a better understanding of its complex effects on the environment and human health.
