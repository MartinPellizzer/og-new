The aim of this systematic review was to investigate the performance of multiple therapeutic approaches for palatal wound healing after soft tissue graft removal. This review aimed to provide an overview of the current evidence on this topic by synthesizing data from systematic reviews that evaluated the effects of low-level laser therapy (LLLT), platelet-rich fibrin (PRF), cyanoacrylate tissue adhesives (CTA), and ozone therapy (OT) on wound healing and postoperative discomfort after palatal soft tissue graft removal.

**Paragraph 2**
The methods used in this systematic review included a comprehensive search of six electronic databases, including PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, and others. The search strategy was designed to capture all relevant studies that evaluated the effects of LLLT, PRF, CTA, and OT on wound healing and postoperative discomfort after palatal soft tissue graft removal. Studies were included if they were systematic reviews that published their protocols and reported the results of at least one randomized clinical trial (RCT). The quality of each study was assessed using the AMSTAR 2 tool.

The results of this systematic review revealed that all four therapies demonstrated improvements in wound healing, with LLLT, PRF, and CTA also reducing pain and analgesic consumption. The discussion of these findings highlights the importance of considering the methodological quality of each study when interpreting the results. Despite the heterogeneity of the studies, the data suggest that LLLT, PRF, CTA, and OT may be effective in improving wound healing and reducing postoperative discomfort after palatal soft tissue graft removal.
