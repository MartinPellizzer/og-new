Tropospheric ozone (O3) is a significant air pollutant that has detrimental effects on human health, plant life, and climate. The formation of O3 involves complex photochemical reactions driven by precursor gases such as oxides of nitrogen (NOx) and volatile organic compounds (VOCs). A key method for quantifying the ozone-forming potential of VOCs is Photochemical Ozone Creation Potential (POCP), which has been compared to alternative methods like Maximum Incremental Reactivity (MIR) and Propene-Equivalent O3 formation weightings. Understanding the mechanisms and quantification of O3 formation is crucial for developing effective strategies to mitigate its adverse impacts on air quality and human health.
