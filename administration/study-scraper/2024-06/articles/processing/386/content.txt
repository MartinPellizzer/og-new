**Paragraph 1: Introduction**
The removal of pollutants from water is a crucial task to ensure the safety and sustainability of our environment. Ozonation is a widely used method for wastewater treatment, but its efficiency can be improved by using catalysts. This study focuses on enhancing the catalytic ozonation process using titanium oxide/zirconium oxide (TiO2/ZrO2) nanoparticles as activated catalysts to generate hydroxyl radicals (OH°). Atrazine was chosen as the pollutant due to its widespread use and persistence in the environment.

**Paragraph 2: Methods**
To synthesize TiO2/ZrO2 nanoparticles, the coprecipitation digestion method was employed. The resulting nanoparticles were characterized using various techniques, including scanning and transmission electron microscopy, X-ray diffraction, Brunauer-Emmett-Teller theory, and energy dispersive X-ray spectroscopy. Experiments were conducted under different initial pH conditions (3, 7, and 10), TiO2/ZrO2 nanocatalyst concentrations (100, 200, and 300 ppm), atrazine concentrations (4.64 × 10−6 M), and ozone concentrations (1.35 × 10−4, 1.55 × 10−4, and 2.27 × 10−4 M).

**Paragraph 3: Results**
The optimal conditions for OH° generation were found to be pH 10, 200 ppm TiO2/ZrO2 concentration, 4.64 × 10−6 M atrazine concentration, and 1.55 × 10−4 M ozone concentration. The TiO2/ZrO2 nanoparticles showed excellent nanocatalytic activity with an Rct value of 2.83 × 10−8. The OH° results obtained under the optimal conditions were approximately 1 × 10−11 mol/L, indicating the potential of TiO2/ZrO2 nanoparticles to enhance OH° generation during catalytic ozonation.

**Paragraph 4: Discussion**
The findings suggest that TiO2/ZrO2 nanoparticles can effectively enhance the ozonation process by promoting the formation of OH° radicals. The optimal conditions achieved in this study can be applied to various wastewater treatment systems. Additionally, the use of TiO2/ZrO2 nanoparticles as catalysts offers a promising solution for the degradation of pollutants in water.

**Paragraph 5: Conclusions**
In conclusion, this study demonstrates the potential of TiO2/ZrO2 nanoparticles as activated catalysts to enhance OH° generation during catalytic ozonation. The optimized conditions achieved in this study can be applied to various wastewater treatment systems, offering a promising solution for the removal of pollutants from water. Further research is needed to explore the scalability and applicability of this technology in real-world settings.
