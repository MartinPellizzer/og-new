**Paragraph 1: Introduction**
The chemical reagent and additive manufacturing industry is a significant contributor to volatile organic compounds (VOCs) emissions in the environment. VOCs have been linked to various environmental and health concerns, including air pollution, climate change, and negative impacts on human health. In Shanghai, China, the chemical reagent and additive manufacturing industry has been identified as a major source of VOC emissions. This study aimed to investigate the emission characteristics of VOCs from six companies in this industry using SUMMA canisters and GC-MS technology.

**Paragraph 2: Methods**
The study employed SUMMA canisters and gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS) technology to collect and analyze VOC samples from six chemical reagent and additive manufacturing companies in Shanghai. The sampling process involved collecting air samples using SUMMA canisters at various locations within each company, followed by analysis using GC-MS instruments. Emission quantities and factors were calculated, and the emission composition spectrum and ozone formation potential (OFP) were analyzed and evaluated.

**Paragraph 3: Results**
The study found that alkanes, aromatics, halogenated hydrocarbons, and oxygen-containing hydrocarbons (OVOCs) accounted for more than 99.5% of total VOC emissions from the chemical reagent and additive manufacturing industry in Shanghai. The major characteristic pollutants identified were 2-methylpentane, 3-methylpentane, n-hexane, methylcyclopentane, tetrahydrofuran, acetone, ethylbenzene, and m/p-xylene. Alkanes, aromatics, and OVOCs were found to be the primary photochemical reactive components, while tetrahydrofuran, 2-methylpentane, 3-methylpentane, 1,2,4-trimethylbenzene, and 1,2,3-trimethylbenzene were identified as the main contributors to OFP.

**Paragraph 4: Discussion**
The study's findings suggest that controlling emissions of C6-C8 alkanes, C8-C9 aromatics, and OVOCs, particularly those with high ozone formation potential, is crucial for reducing the industry's environmental impact. The source reactivity (SR) of this industry was found to be within the average range of various industries, indicating that control measures should focus on the identified priority pollutants. The study highlights the need for stricter regulations and enforcement to reduce VOC emissions from the chemical reagent and additive manufacturing industry in Shanghai.

**Paragraph 5: Conclusions**
In conclusion, this study provides valuable insights into the emission characteristics of VOCs from the chemical reagent and additive manufacturing industry in Shanghai. The findings emphasize the importance of controlling emissions of priority pollutants to mitigate their impact on the environment and human health. The study suggests that policy makers and regulatory authorities should prioritize reducing emissions of C6-C8 alkanes, C8-C9 aromatics, and OVOCs, particularly those with high ozone formation potential, to minimize the industry's environmental footprint.
