The quality of air is a crucial aspect of human health and well-being. Air pollution is a significant concern in many urban areas, including Chimbote, Peru. The Organismo de Evaluación y Fiscalización Ambiental (OEFA) conducted a monitoring study to assess the quality of air in Chimbote from June 5th to 11th, 2014.

**Paragraph 2**
The monitoring study was carried out in El Trapecio urbanization, District of Chimbote, Ancash Department. The authors, Chinen Guima and colleagues, used high-volume samplers to collect particulate matter (PM10) and gas samples from June 5th to 11th, 2014. The sampling stations were located at the center of the urban area, near traffic arteries, and in areas with industrial activities.

**Paragraph 3**
The results showed that the concentrations of PM10, Sulfur Hydrogen (H2S), Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2), Carbon Monoxide (CO), and Tropospheric Ozone (O3) did not exceed the National Ambient Air Quality Standard. However, the concentration of Sulfur Dioxide (SO2) exceeded the standard on June 6th, with a daily average of 24.1 μg/m3.

The findings suggest that while some air pollutants do not pose an immediate threat to public health in Chimbote, others may still be a concern.
