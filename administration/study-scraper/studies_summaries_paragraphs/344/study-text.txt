In Lhasa, China, ozone (O3) production sensitivity was investigated by measuring direct O3 production rates (OPR). The study found that Lhasa exhibited higher OPR with reduced variations compared to Beijing, attributed to stronger UVR and OH reactivity influenced by oxidized volatile organic compounds. A less pronounced NO turning point in OPR was observed in Lhasa, favoring a daytime peroxy radical production regime and highlighting the need for continued monitoring and research to develop effective control strategies.
