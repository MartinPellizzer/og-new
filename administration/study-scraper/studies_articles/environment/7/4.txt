In conclusion, the present long-term study reveals that Delhi's air quality is severely polluted, exceeding both national and global guidelines. The high concentrations of PM10 and PM2.5 pose a significant threat to public health, particularly in industrial areas. The study highlights the need for immediate attention to reduce emissions from industries and vehicles to improve air quality in Delhi. The results also emphasize the importance of using AirQ+ model for health risk assessment of air pollution to inform policy decisions.
