**Paragraph 1**
Ground-level ozone (O3) pollution has become a significant concern in urban agglomerations, driven by meteorological conditions and social-economic factors. This study aims to comprehensively reveal the spatial distribution and driving mechanism of O3 pollution in the Yangtze River Delta (YRD), a region with rapid economic development and population growth.

**Paragraph 2**
The authors constructed a comprehensive analysis framework combining spatial analysis and spatial econometric regression using O3 monitoring data from 41 cities in the YRD from 2014 to 2021. The study employed a spatial econometric model to investigate the effects of urbanization, traffic conditions, energy consumption, and meteorological factors on O3 concentrations.

**Paragraph 3**
The results showed that O3 concentrations in the YRD exhibited an increasing trend until 2018 and then decreased slightly. Cities with higher O3 concentrations were primarily concentrated in central and southern Jiangsu, Shanghai, and northern Zhejiang. The effects of urbanization on O3 concentrations followed an inverted U-shaped curve, implying that achieving high-quality urbanization is essential for effectively controlling urban O3 pollution.

**Paragraph 4**
The study also found that traffic conditions and energy consumption have significant direct positive influences on O3 concentrations and spatial spillover effects. Additionally, the authors suggested that addressing overall regional energy consumption and implementing traffic source regulations are crucial paths for O3 pollution control in the YRD. Meteorological conditions, such as wind speed, play a certain role in regulating O3 concentrations.

**Paragraph 5**
In conclusion, this study provides valuable insights into the spatial distribution and driving mechanism of O3 pollution in the Yangtze River Delta. The results highlight the importance of considering both urbanization and traffic/energy-related factors when designing effective policies to improve air quality and mitigate ozone pollution in urban agglomerations. The findings of this study can inform policy-making decisions aimed at reducing O3 pollution and improving public health in the YRD region.
