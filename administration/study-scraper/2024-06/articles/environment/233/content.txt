The researchers constructed a retrospective cohort using California's fee-for-service Medicare beneficiaries from 2016-18 who had undergone incisional glaucoma surgery (trabeculectomy, tube shunt, or EX-PRESS shunt). The primary exposures were annual-average outdoor concentrations of three air pollutants: carbon monoxide (CO), fine particulate matter with a diameter of 2.5 µm or less (PM2.5), and ozone (O3). Outdoor pollutant concentration was estimated using land use regression models and averaged over the zip code for each beneficiary's mailing address.

The analysis revealed that exposure to increased concentrations of outdoor air pollutants was associated with greater risk for incisional glaucoma surgical failure.
