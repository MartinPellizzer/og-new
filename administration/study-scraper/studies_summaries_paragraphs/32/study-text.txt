Here is a summary of the study in 1 detailed paragraph, focusing on information and data related to ozone:

The study analyzed atmospheric air pollution in the Voronezh Region from 2020-2022 and found that ozone was one of the substances with high average daily concentrations at 13 monitoring posts. The concentration of ozone was among other pollutants such as carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, nitrogen dioxide, suspended solids, and phenol, all emanating from stationary and mobile sources. Notably, the study also monitored the levels of four carcinogenic substances: lead, styrene, formaldehyde, and soot.
