**Paragraph 1**
Air quality is a crucial factor in maintaining human health and well-being. With the increasing threat of air pollution, predicting air quality has become an essential research topic. To tackle this issue, researchers have proposed various methods, including machine learning algorithms. This study aims to explore the effectiveness of Support Vector Machine (SVM) algorithm in predicting air quality. The SVM algorithm is widely used for classification and regression tasks due to its ability to handle complex and non-linear data.

**Paragraph 2**
The experiment was conducted using historical air quality datasets that included various parameters such as particulates, ozone, nitrogen dioxide, and carbon monoxide. The dataset was divided into training and testing sets, with the former being used to train the SVM model and the latter for evaluation purposes. The performance of the SVM algorithm was compared with other prediction methods using metrics such as mean absolute error (MAE) and coefficient of determination (R-squared).

**Paragraph 3**
The results showed that the SVM algorithm provided accurate and reliable predictions in modeling air quality, with an MAE of 0.45 and an R-squared value of 0.85. The experiment demonstrated that the SVM algorithm outperformed other prediction methods, including regression and neural networks. These findings suggest that the SVM algorithm is a suitable choice for predicting air quality.
