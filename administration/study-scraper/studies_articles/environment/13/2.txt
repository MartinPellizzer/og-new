The results showed that maternal age, pregnancy times, number of births, number of cesarean sections, season of conception, complications diseases, comorbidities diseases, hypertension disorder of pregnancy, and neonatal low birth weight of the newborn were significantly different between preterm and term pregnant women. After adjusting for confounders, logistic regression analysis revealed that each 10 Î¼g/m3 increase in PM2.5, PM10, NO2 concentrations during mid-pregnancy (T2) was associated with a 0.9%, 0.6%, and 2.4% increased risk of preterm birth, respectively.
