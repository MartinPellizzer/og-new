In conclusion, this case study in Taiwan highlights the country's progress in reducing ODS consumption over the last 30 years. The study demonstrates the effectiveness of regulatory actions and industry efforts in achieving phase-out targets. To mitigate global warming, Taiwan has implemented measures to reduce HFC emissions, aiming for an 80% reduction by 2045. This experience can serve as a model for other countries to follow in their efforts to protect the ozone layer and combat climate change.
