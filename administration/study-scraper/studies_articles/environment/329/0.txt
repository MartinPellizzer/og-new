Inhalers play a crucial role in managing respiratory diseases, but their environmental impact has gained increasing attention. As family physicians, it is essential to consider the carbon footprint of our prescribing decisions. The Climate-Conscious Inhaler Prescribing Sustainable Pathway, recently published in Canadian Family Physician, provides a helpful framework for environmentally conscious inhaler prescribing. However, there are limitations and uncertainties surrounding the actual carbon impact of switching from metered dose inhalers (MDIs) to dry powder inhalers (DPIs).
