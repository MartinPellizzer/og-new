In conclusion, this study demonstrates the potential of photocatalytic processes integrating magnetic nanocomposites for micropollutant removal from wastewater. The results highlight the importance of reactor design and operational conditions in optimizing the performance of the process. The use of TiO2 or ZnO as catalysts offers a promising solution for efficient and cost-effective removal of micropollutants from wastewater, paving the way for large-scale implementation and real-world application.
