The results showed that the March 2024 Arctic TCO set a record high value of 478 Dobson Units (DU), which is about 60 DU higher than average and 6 DU higher than the previous record in March 1979. The daily TCO measurements were above average for every day in March 2024, and record highs were observed from 11 to 26 March 2024. Furthermore, the MLS data revealed that the record ozone anomaly was concentrated in the lower stratosphere (10-30 km).
